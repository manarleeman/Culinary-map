About Gizdodo

Gizdodo, a portmanteau of "gizzard" and "dodo" (fried plantain), is a beloved Nigerian dish that has become a staple at parties, gatherings, and restaurants across the country. This innovative dish combines the satisfying chewiness of chicken gizzards with the sweet, starchy goodness of fried plantains, all brought together in a flavorful, spicy sauce.

The dish originated in the early 2000s as Nigerian cuisine began to evolve and experiment with fusion flavors. It's particularly popular in Lagos and other urban centers, where it's served as both an appetizer and a main course. The contrast between the tender-crisp gizzards and soft plantains creates a textural masterpiece that keeps people coming back for more.

Gizdodo exemplifies how Nigerian cuisine continues to innovate while maintaining its core identity of bold flavors and satisfying textures. The harmonious marriage of protein and plantain creates a balanced meal that's as nutritious as it is delicious.

Ingredients

Everything you need to prepare delicious Gizdodo

For the Gizzards

  • 500g Chicken gizzards, cleaned and trimmed
  • 1 Onion, diced (divided use)
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 2 Bouillon/stock cubes
  • 1 tsp Curry powder
  • 1 tsp Thyme
  • 1 tsp Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp Black pepper
  • 2 cups Water (for boiling)

For the Plantain (Dodo)

  • 3 Ripe plantains (yellow with black spots)
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 2 cups Vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Sauce

  • 3 tbsp Vegetable oil
  • 1 Large onion, finely diced
  • 2 Red bell peppers, diced
  • 1 Green bell pepper, diced
  • 2-3 Scotch bonnet peppers, finely diced (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Tomato paste
  • 2 Garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 inch Ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp Dried thyme
  • 1 tsp Curry powder
  • 1 Bouillon/stock cube
  • Salt and pepper To taste
  • 2 tbsp Chopped fresh cilantro or parsley (for garnish)

üí° Ingredient Tips

  • When buying gizzards, try to get them pre-cleaned to save time, but still rinse thoroughly.
  • Choose plantains that are ripe but still firm - yellow with black spots is perfect.
  • For a more colorful dish, use a mix of red, yellow, and green bell peppers.
  • Adjust the scotch bonnet peppers based on your heat preference.

üîÑ Substitutions

  • Chicken livers can be used instead of gizzards for a softer texture.
  • Turkey gizzards work well as a substitute but may require longer cooking time.
  • Sweet potatoes can replace plantains for a different flavor profile.
  • Jalape√±os or habaneros can be used if scotch bonnet peppers are unavailable.

Preparation Method

Step-by-step guide to perfect Gizdodo

1

Clean the Gizzards

Thoroughly clean the gizzards by removing any yellow membrane or debris. Rinse several times under cold water until the water runs clear.

Clean gizzards are essential for the best flavor. If they came pre-cleaned, still rinse them well.
2

Boil the Gizzards

In a pot, add the gizzards, half of the diced onion, bay leaves, 1 stock cube, thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper. Add water to cover and boil for 25-30 minutes until tender but still slightly chewy.

Gizzards should be cooked until tender but not mushy. Test one by cutting into it - it should offer some resistance but not be rubbery.
3

Prepare the Plantains

Peel the plantains and cut into 1-inch cubes. Sprinkle with salt and set aside for 5 minutes.

Consistent cube sizes ensure even cooking for the plantains.
4

Fry the Plantains

Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry the plantain cubes in batches until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels.

Don't overcrowd the pan when frying to ensure each piece gets crispy on the outside and soft inside.
5

Fry the Gizzards

Once the gizzards are cooked, drain and pat dry. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and quickly fry the gizzards until they develop a golden-brown crust, about 3-5 minutes. Remove and set aside.

This quick frying adds texture and seals in the flavors of the boiled gizzards.
6

Prepare the Sauce Base

In a large pan, heat 3 tablespoons of oil. Add the remaining onions and saut√© until translucent. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for 30 seconds until fragrant.

Don't burn the garlic and ginger as it will make the sauce bitter.
7

Complete the Sauce

Add the diced bell peppers and scotch bonnet peppers, cooking for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another 2 minutes to remove the raw taste.

Cooking the tomato paste is crucial to develop rich flavor in the sauce.
8

Season the Sauce

Add the thyme, curry powder, remaining stock cube, salt, and pepper. Stir well and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes to blend the flavors.

Taste the sauce at this point and adjust seasonings if needed.
9

Combine Everything

Add the fried gizzards to the sauce, stirring to coat evenly. Cook for 2-3 minutes to let the gizzards absorb some of the sauce. Then gently fold in the fried plantains, being careful not to mash them.

Folding in the plantains at the end helps them maintain their texture while still absorbing flavor.
10

Serve

Turn off the heat and let the dish rest for 2 minutes. Garnish with chopped cilantro or parsley and serve hot as a main course or appetizer.

Gizdodo is best served immediately while the plantains still have some crispness to contrast with the sauce.

Serving Suggestions

How to enjoy your Gizdodo like a true Nigerian

Traditional Pairings

Gizdodo is versatile and can be served in various ways:

  • As an Appetizer: Serve in small portions at parties or gatherings.
  • With Rice: Particularly delicious alongside Jollof rice or white rice.
  • With Pasta: Works wonderfully as a sauce for spaghetti or other pasta.
  • Standalone Dish: Enjoyed on its own as a complete meal.

Accompaniments

To elevate your Gizdodo experience, consider serving with:

  • Chilled Drinks: Cold Chapman, zobo (hibiscus drink), or beer.
  • Side Salad: A simple cucumber and tomato salad adds freshness.
  • Fried Plantain Chips: For extra plantain goodness.
  • Pepper Sauce: For those who want to add extra heat.

Serving Occasions

Gizdodo is particularly associated with:

  • Parties and Celebrations: A popular feature at Nigerian parties and gatherings.
  • Weekend Meals: Perfect for a special Saturday or Sunday lunch.
  • Small Chops: Often included in the "small chops" selection at events.
  • Restaurant Menus: A staple in many Nigerian restaurants and bukaterias.

Cultural Significance

The story behind Gizdodo

Gizdodo represents the innovative spirit of modern Nigerian cuisine. Unlike more traditional dishes with centuries of history, Gizdodo emerged in the early 2000s as creative chefs and home cooks began experimenting with combining popular elements of Nigerian food in new ways.

The dish showcases Nigeria's "no waste" culinary philosophy by elevating gizzards‚Äîan often overlooked part of the chicken‚Äîinto a star ingredient. This resourcefulness is a hallmark of Nigerian cooking, where every part of an animal or plant is utilized.

The name itself‚Äîa blend of "gizzard" and "dodo" (the Nigerian term for fried plantains)‚Äîreflects the playful linguistic creativity common in Nigerian food culture. The dish has quickly become a staple at parties, particularly in Lagos, Nigeria's culinary melting pot.

Tips & Variations

Master the art of Gizdodo

Chef's Tips

  • For more tender gizzards, boil them with a tablespoon of apple cider vinegar.
  • Fry the plantains until they're just golden brown - overcooked plantains will become mushy in the sauce.
  • Cut the gizzards into bite-sized pieces after boiling for easier eating and better sauce absorption.
  • Add the fried plantains at the very last minute to maintain their texture.
  • Reserve some of the gizzard stock to add to the sauce if it becomes too thick.

Regional Variations

Lagos Style

Often includes more bell peppers and sometimes incorporates shrimp for a coastal twist.

Eastern Nigerian Version

Sometimes includes uziza (piper guineense) leaves or seeds for a distinctive peppery flavor.

Modern Fusion

Contemporary chefs have created versions with coconut milk for a creamier sauce or added smoked fish for extra umami.
