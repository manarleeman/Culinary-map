Niger Delta Cuisine

Experience the diverse and seafood-rich culinary traditions from Nigeria's coastal South South region

Cultural Context

The South South region, also known as the Niger Delta, encompasses diverse ethnic groups including the Ijaw, Ibibio, Efik, Urhobo, Itsekiri, and many others. This cultural diversity is reflected in the region's rich and varied cuisine.

With its coastal geography and extensive network of rivers, the Niger Delta's culinary identity is deeply rooted in its abundant seafood and freshwater resources. Fishing is a traditional occupation, and seafood forms the foundation of many local dishes.

Traditional preservation methods such as smoking, drying, and fermentation are widely practiced, not only to extend shelf life but also to enhance flavor.
